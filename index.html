Audio Research: Automatic Lyric Display System

During the summer of 2016 I was awarded a research fellowship to create an automatic lyrics display system in Java under the guidance of Prof. Zhiyao Duan, in the Audio Information Research Lab at the University of Rochester. The primary purpose of my research was to design a computational system that could follow live musical performances and display pre-encoded lyrics in real-time. I implemented a multi-threaded audio framework with a shared synchronous buffer to handle simultaneous recording and processing. As basic harmonic progression of two recordings is similar, I extracted chroma feature vectors from the waveform audio data - the chroma feature uses 12 bins to represent the relative energy of the audio in the 12 semitones of a musical octave, and represents the harmonic content of the audio. I then used online dynamic time warping to align the live audio stream with a pre-annotated version of the audio based on their harmonic similarity, and display lyrics in real-time.
