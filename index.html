Audio Research: Automatic Lyrics Display System

I was awarded a research fellowship to create an automatic lyrics display system in Java under the guidance of Prof. Zhiyao Duan, in the Audio Information Research Lab at the University of Rochester during the summer of 2016. The primary purpose of this research was to design a computational system that could follow live musical performances and display pre-encoded lyrics in real-time. I implemented a multi-threaded audio framework with a shared synchronous buffer to handle simultaneous recording and processing. I extracted chroma feature vectors from the waveform audio data and then used online dynamic time warping to align the live audio stream with a pre-annotated version of the audio based on their harmonic similarity, and display lyrics in real-time.
